## Spider-Man (1967) – Season 1 Episodes (Readable Format)

### 1a. The Power of Dr Octopus

**September 9, 1967**
The Power of Dr. Octopus

Teenage photographer Peter Parker is sent by the Daily Bugle to investigate a series of mysterious lights in a wooded area outside the city. On 
the way there, a sudden landslide forces him off the road and leaves his car stuck in a tree.

Realizing he is in danger, Peter slides down in the driver’s seat out of sight, then rises back up moments later after making a quick change into 
Spider-Man. He lowers the car safely to the ground and discovers the hidden entrance to a secret laboratory operated by his enemy, Doctor Octopus.

Inside, Spider-Man is captured, and Doctor Octopus reveals his plan to blow up part of New York City. Octopus has already mailed a warning letter 
to the Daily Bugle so citizens can evacuate before his demonstration, but J. Jonah Jameson dismisses it as a crank letter.

Spider-Man breaks free, but is soon trapped again when a cage is dropped over him. Meanwhile, Betty Brant drives to the same location Peter had 
been investigating and is captured as well.

Spider-Man escapes the cage, only to be knocked unconscious and captured again. Eventually, he manages to free both himself and Betty, then 
defeats Doctor Octopus and stops the plot in time, saving the city from destruction.

## Spider-Man (1967) – Season 1 Episodes Continued

### 16a. The Vulture's Prey

**December 23, 1967**
J. Jonah Jameson investigates a malfunctioning tower clock and is captured by the Vulture, who is using it as a hideout while committing crimes. The Vulture steals a military rocket. Peter realizes the clock chimes are a cry for help, becomes Spider-Man, rescues Jameson, and defeats the Vulture. 

### 16b. The Dark Terrors

**December 23, 1967**
Spider-Man rescues Betty Brant from a shadow tiger but discovers the Phantom is creating living shadows to commit crimes. Spider-Man tracks the shadow creatures to a warehouse and defeats the Phantom. 

### 17a. The Terrible Triumph of Dr Octopus

**December 30, 1967**
Doctor Octopus steals Dr. Smartyr’s Nullifier weapon. Spider-Man plants a tracer, tracks Octopus to the waterfront, survives an explosive trap, and paralyzes Octopus with electricity long enough for police to arrest him. 

### 17b. Magic Malice

**December 30, 1967**
The Green Goblin steals magician Blackwell’s secrets and uses tricks to rob the city. Spider-Man is trapped in chains inside a water-filled tank but escapes using knowledge from Blackwell’s book, then defeats the Goblin with Blackwell’s help. 

### 18a. Fountain of Terror

**January 6, 1968**
Peter learns Dr. Connors has vanished again. In Florida, Spider-Man battles Harley Clivendon while searching the jungle for Connors. They discover an ancient conquistador guarding the Fountain of Youth. Clivendon is defeated, and the fountain is destroyed. 

### 18b. Fiddler on the Loose

**January 6, 1968**
The Fiddler uses sonic violin blasts to attack Spider-Man and extort millionaire Cyrus Flintridge III. After increasing his ransom demands, the Fiddler is defeated by Spider-Man and identified as Otto. 

### 19a. To Catch a Spider

**January 13, 1968**
Dr. Noah Boddy frees Electro, the Green Goblin, and the Vulture so they can all take revenge on Spider-Man. They disable Spider-Man’s powers, but he tricks them into fighting one another at the docks, then webs up Boddy and the villains for arrest. 

### 19b. Double Identity

**January 13, 1968**
Spider-Man chases a criminal disguised as Peter Parker who steals a valuable manuscript. The culprit is master impersonator Charles Cameo, aided by Brutus. After Cameo impersonates Spider-Man during a police trap, the real Spider-Man captures both criminals. 

## Spider-Man (1967) – Season 1 Episodes Continued

### 20a. Sting of the Scorpion

**January 20, 1968**
The Scorpion escapes prison through a tunnel to get revenge on J. Jonah Jameson and Spider-Man. He drinks a toxic chemical that greatly increases his strength, but it also causes him to grow into a giant monster. After capturing Jameson, the Scorpion is forced to drink an antidote by Spider-Man, shrinking him back to normal before being webbed up for police. 

### 20b. Trick or Treachery

**January 20, 1968**
The Human Flies, Stan and Lee Patterson, are released from prison. They resume crime by framing Spider-Man, using one twin disguised as Spider-Man while secretly tracking the real hero with a fly-tracer. Spider-Man discovers the trick, removes the tracer, and captures both twins.

## Spider-Man (1967) – Season 2 Begins

### 21. The Origin of Spider-Man

**September 14, 1968**
Peter Parker is bitten by a radioactive spider and gains amazing spider-like abilities. After selfishly allowing a thief to escape, he later learns the criminal murdered Uncle Ben. Peter captures the thief and realizes that with great power comes great responsibility. 

### 22. King Pinned

**September 21, 1968**
Aunt May becomes ill, and Peter takes a job at the Daily Bugle to help pay for medicine. He uncovers a counterfeit drug ring run by the Kingpin. After Jameson is kidnapped and the Bugle is threatened by a time bomb, Spider-Man rescues Jameson, saves the presses, and breaks up the criminal operation. 

### 23. Swing City

**September 28, 1968**
A mad scientist known as the Master Technician seizes control of a nuclear reactor and lifts Manhattan into the sky with anti-gravity forces. Spider-Man battles through anti-radiation traps, reaches the reactor, and defeats the villain before the city crashes. 

### 24. Criminals in the Clouds

**October 5, 1968**
Peter tries out for the football team but is made waterboy instead. Meanwhile, the Skymaster kidnaps star quarterback Roy Robinson to ransom his scientist father. Spider-Man infiltrates the villain’s hidden airship, rescues Roy, and crashes the flying base into the harbor. 

### 25. Menace from the Bottom of the World

**October 12, 1968**
Peter investigates mysterious underground voices and disappearing banks. Spider-Man descends into a hidden world of Molemen, where he discovers escaped criminal Mugs Reily posing as their leader. Once exposed, the Molemen turn on Reily and restore the stolen banks. 

### 26. Diamond Dust

**October 19, 1968**
Peter hopes to earn a place on the baseball team while thieves led by Shakespeare steal a massive diamond using gorilla disguises and liquid latex weapons. Spider-Man stops the robbery, defeats Shakespeare, and still returns in time to throw the winning pitch. 

### 27. Spider-Man Battles the Molemen

**October 26, 1968**
Spider-Man is dragged underground again by the Molemen, who plan to conquer the surface. He escapes dwarves, giants, and traps, captures their true leader called the Mole, and forces the stolen buildings to be returned. 

### 28. Phantom from the Depths of Time

**November 2, 1968**
Spider-Man answers a distress signal from an island ruled by the alien Dr. Manta, who enslaves the population with robotic beetles. After defeating the beetles and destroying the Mountain Monster, Spider-Man frees the islanders. 

### 29. The Evil Sorcerer

**November 9, 1968**
An ancient magician named Kotep the Scarlet Sorcerer is revived in modern times. Seeking world conquest, he summons demons and traps Spider-Man in his castle. Spider-Man ultimately destroys Kotep’s magical scepter, ending his power. 

### 30. Vine

**November 16, 1968**
Peter accidentally unleashes a giant plant monster and travels back three million years to find the missing scientist who can stop it. In a prehistoric world of blue giants and intelligent plant creatures, Spider-Man steals radium gems, destroys the plant kingdom, and uses the gems to wither the giant plant in the present. 

## Spider-Man (1967) – Season 2 Continued

### 31. Pardo Presents

**November 23, 1968**
While attending a movie premiere with Polly, Peter encounters the criminal Pardo, who can transform into pure energy and take the form of a giant black cat. Theater patrons are hypnotized while Pardo’s gang robs them. Spider-Man resists the hypnosis, escapes several traps, and defeats Pardo atop the Brooklyn Bridge. 

### 32. Cloud City of Gold

**November 30, 1968**
As an exchange student in South America, Peter’s plane crashes in the Andes during a storm. Spider-Man leads the survivors through jungles, caves, and underground rivers to the legendary Cloud City of Gold, ruled by conquistador DeVargas. After battling giant monsters and freeing his companions, Spider-Man escapes as the volcano containing the city erupts. 

### 33. Neptune's Nose Cone

**December 7, 1968**
Peter is sent to Antarctica to photograph a crashed lunar module. He and pilot Penny crash-land on an island where natives plan to sacrifice Penny and the spacecraft nose cone to their volcano god. Spider-Man battles strange creatures, saves Penny, and prevents disaster by webbing the nose cone before it falls into the volcano. 

### 34. Home

**December 14, 1968**
Peter meets Carol, a girl who seems to share his scientific interests, but later discovers she is committing robberies using spider-like powers. Spider-Man follows her to a hidden alien civilization living underground. Their people are trying to stop a proton experiment that would destroy them, and Spider-Man saves them so they can finally return home. 

### 35. Blotto

**December 21, 1968**
Former film producer Clive creates a monstrous creature named Blotto using a device called the Spirit-Scope, which can bring images to life. Blotto begins consuming buildings and objects across the city. Spider-Man defeats Clive, seizes the machine, and turns Blotto back into harmless film-paper. 

### 36. Thunder Rumble

**December 28, 1968**
Spider-Man battles Bolton, a Martian warrior who can create and throw lightning bolts while trying to steal Earth’s gold. With help from crook Boomer, Bolton nearly succeeds, but Spider-Man tricks him into blasting himself back into space and captures Boomer. 

### 37. Spider-Man Meets Skyboy

**January 4, 1969**
Scientist Dr. Caldwell is kidnapped by Dr. Zapp, who wants the Astro-Helmet invention. Caldwell’s son Jan becomes Skyboy using the helmet and his father’s flight suit. Spider-Man helps Skyboy rescue Dr. Caldwell and defeat Dr. Zapp on Lightning Mountain. 

### 38. Cold Storage

**January 11, 1969**
Spider-Man pursues jewel thief Dr. Cool but is frozen inside a deep-freeze chamber. He hallucinates waking in a ruined future world full of prehistoric monsters before realizing it was a dream caused by the freezing process. Once recovered, Spider-Man returns and defeats Dr. Cool with a load of ice. 

### 39. To Cage a Spider

**January 18, 1969**
Spider-Man is injured during a chase and taken to a prison infirmary by Captain Ned Stacy. When inmates stage a breakout and take Stacy hostage, Spider-Man pretends to help them, then defeats the prisoners one by one in the dark and saves the captain. 

## Spider-Man (1967) – Season 3 Episodes

### 40b. Conner's Reptiles

**March 22, 1970**
An alligator gains superhuman intelligence through Dr. Connors’ experiments, kidnaps him, and plans to create an army of enhanced reptiles to rule the swamps. Spider-Man uses the same serum against the creature and defeats it. 

### 41a. Trouble With Snow

**March 29, 1970**
A gigantic living snowman is accidentally created through chemical reactions and electrical energy. It grows larger with each snowfall. Spider-Man uses a copper cable to drain away its power and melts it. 

### 41b. Spider-Man vs. Desperado

**March 29, 1970**
Spider-Man battles Desperado, a cowboy criminal who rides a flying electronic horse and uses hypnotic guns during bank robberies. Spider-Man lures him away from the horse and captures him. 

### 42a. Sky Harbor

**April 5, 1970**
Baron von Rantenraven commands a giant airborne fortress crewed by sky pirates. Using biplanes and dust clouds, they attack aircraft and New York City. Spider-Man tricks the Baron into crashing into the zeppelins supporting Sky Harbor, bringing the fortress down. 

### 42b. The Big Brainwasher

**April 5, 1970**
Mary Jane Watson is hired as a dancer at a nightclub secretly being used by the Kingpin to mind-control city officials. Spider-Man escapes a deadly trap, rescues Mary Jane, and defeats the Kingpin. This is Mary Jane’s only appearance in the original series. 

### 43a. The Vanishing Doctor Vespasian

**April 12, 1970**
Dr. Vespasian invents an invisibility serum and uses it on himself and his dog Brutus while plotting to dominate the city’s criminals. Spider-Man defeats the invisible dog and later exposes Vespasian by dropping ice cream onto him during a jewel robbery. 

### 43b. The Scourge of the Scarf

**April 12, 1970**
A thief known as the Scarf uses illusions and elaborate tricks to rob wealthy citizens. During a museum robbery, Spider-Man alerts police and defeats the Scarf while the gang is captured. 

### 44a. Super Swami

**April 19, 1970**
A fake mystic called the Super Swami uses hypnosis and stage tricks to cause citywide chaos, including fake snowstorms and disappearing bridges. Spider-Man exposes him as a fraud and turns him over to police. 

### 44b. The Birth of Micro Man

**April 19, 1970**
Escaped criminal Professor Pretorius creates the Kingdom Come Machine and can shrink himself to tiny size. Spider-Man must shrink as well to battle him, then destroys the machine with water and clears Peter Parker’s name. 

### 45a. Knight Must Fall

**April 26, 1970**
Spider-Man faces Sir Galahad, a modern criminal knight who rides a motorcycle and uses an electrified lance. Spider-Man defeats him during a jousting battle near the waterfront. 

### 45b. The Devious Dr Dumpty

**April 26, 1970**
Dr. Humperdink Dumpty uses balloons, laughing gas, and parade chaos to commit robberies. Spider-Man survives their balloon escape and later captures Dumpty and his gang during another heist. 

### 46. Up from Nowhere

**May 3, 1970**
Dr. Atlantean places Manhattan beneath the ocean inside a giant dome so Atlantis can invade the surface world. Spider-Man infiltrates his submarine, turns the villain’s weapons against him, restores the city, and saves New York. 

### 47. Rollarama

**May 10, 1970**
Giant rolling seed pods from another dimension threaten the city. Spider-Man travels through a portal, battles blue giants and intelligent plant creatures, steals their radium power source, and uses it to destroy the pods. 

### 48a. Rhino

**May 17, 1970**
The Rhino again seeks to build a golden statue of himself by stealing the city’s remaining gold. Spider-Man captures him while he tries escaping through the sewers. 

### 48b. The Madness of Mysterio

**May 17, 1970**
Mysterio tricks Spider-Man into believing he has shrunk to six inches tall and traps him in an abandoned amusement park. Spider-Man realizes it is hypnosis, finds Mysterio, and captures him. 

### 49. Revolt in the Fifth Dimension

**May 24, 1970**
A tiny alien scientist from the doomed world Gorth entrusts Spider-Man with a knowledge orb called the Library. The villain Infinata drags Spider-Man into the Fifth Dimension, where fear powers his illusions. Spider-Man defeats him by refusing to fear anything and escapes. 

### 50. Specialists and Slaves

**May 31, 1970**
The Radiation Specialist escapes prison and again levitates Manhattan while using mind-control radiation on the population. Spider-Man avoids capture, rescues Captain Stacy, and defeats the Specialist once more. 

### 51. Down to Earth

**June 7, 1970**
A meteorite crashes on an island where Peter and pilot Osa also crash-land. Natives plan to sacrifice Osa and the meteorite in a volcano, but Spider-Man intervenes and discovers the meteorite is actually a spaceship. 

### 52. Trip to Tomorrow

**June 14, 1970**
Spider-Man meets a runaway boy hiding in a boxcar and tells stories of his past adventures to inspire him. The boy decides to return home instead of running away, and Spider-Man laughs as the train departs. 









1a	1a	"The Power of Dr Octopus"	September 9, 1967	1
Teenage photographer Peter Parker is sent by the Daily Bugle to investigate a series of mystery lights in a wooded area outside of the city, when 
a sudden landslide forces him to swerve off the road and land in a tree. Realizing he is in a precarious spot, Peter slides down in the driver's 
seat and out of sight, only to rise back up again as he finishes a quick change into Spider-Man. He lowers the car to the ground and finds the 
hidden entrance to a secret laboratory operated by his nemesis, Doctor Octopus, who captures the young hero and reveals his plan to blow up part 
of New York City. Octopus has also sent a warning letter to the Daily Bugle about it, so that people can evacuate before the demonstration, but 
J. Jonah Jameson thinks it is just a crank letter. Spider-Man breaks free, but is caught when a cage is dropped onto him. Betty Brant drives off 
to the place that Peter was heading to, but she is also captured. Spider-Man escapes his cage, but is knocked out and captured again. Spider-Man 
manages to free both himself and Betty, then stops the nefarious villain in his tracks, saving the city from destruction.

Season 2 (1968–69)

After Grantray-Lawrence Animation went bankrupt, Krantz Animation, Inc. took over production for the second season of Spider-Man. Krantz 
Animation turned to Ralph Bakshi to take over executive producing and directing the series, which involved moving production to New York City. 
Bakshi wanted a series that "offered more attention to detail" and allowed "for a greater emphasis on character and plotline."[1][2] Animation 
was performed by Clifford Augustson, Douglas Crane, and Frank Enders, among others, and Ira Turek, Lin Carter, and Fred Halliday were the 
writers.[1]

Unlike the first-season episodes, all second-season episodes consisted of a single story.
