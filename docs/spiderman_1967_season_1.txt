Spider-Man (1967) – Season 1 Episode Summaries

1.  The Power of Dr. Octopus 
    Villain: Doctor Octopus 

    Doctor Octopus demonstrates a powerful nuclear device and plans to use it for
    blackmail. Spider-Man investigates and is captured, learning
    firsthand how dangerous Doc Ock’s mechanical arms are. Using quick
    thinking and agility, he escapes captivity. He ultimately disables
    the device and stops Octopus before the plan can unfold.

2.  Sub-Zero for Spidey 
    Villain: Plutonians

    On a blistering hot afternoon, Peter is walking to the house of a brilliant scientist, 
    Doctor Smartyr, when suddenly he notices that the sidewalk and most of the neighborhood is turning to ice. 
    Hearing desperate cries for help from Smartyr's house, Peter changes into Spider-Man and breaks through 
    a laboratory window to find the frightened scientist being menaced by a towering ice creature. 
    It leaves, and Spider-Man finds a giant iceberg in New York Harbor. 
    He returns to Dr Smartyr, who deduces that the ice creature comes from Pluto, 
    but more ice creatures arrive, freezing him and Smartyr and kidnapping the scientist. T
    he ice creatures actually do turn out to be 'Plutonians' and are not hostile. 
    They are seeking out Dr Smartyr for a way to help them get back to their home planet.

    
3.  Where Crawls the Lizard 
    Villain: The Lizard 

    Dr. Curt Connors transforms into the Lizard and retreats into the swamps, threatening
    nearby communities. Spider-Man tracks him down and tries to reason
    with him. The Lizard’s animal instincts overpower his humanity,
    making him highly dangerous. Spider-Man eventually finds a way to
    reverse the transformation.

4.  Electro the Human Lightning Bolt 
    Villain: Electro 
    
    Electro gains the ability to travel through electrical systems and begins robbing
    banks. Spider-Man struggles to fight an enemy who can’t be
    physically contained easily. Electro uses the city’s power grid to
    stay one step ahead. Spider-Man ultimately traps him using
    insulation and clever tactics.

5.  The Menace of Mysterio 
    Villain: Mysterio 
    
    Mysterio frames Spider-Man
    for crimes to boost his own fame as a “hero.” Using illusions and
    special effects, he tricks the public and authorities. Spider-Man
    must clear his name while uncovering how the illusions work. He
    exposes Mysterio’s deception and defeats him publicly.

6.  The Sky Is Falling 
    Villain: Vulture 
    
    The Vulture uses trained birds
    to carry out coordinated attacks and thefts. Spider-Man must deal
    with both the villain and his airborne army. The attacks create
    widespread panic in the city. Spider-Man defeats the Vulture in an
    aerial showdown.

7.  Captured by J. Jonah Jameson 
    Villain: J. Jonah Jameson (misguided)

    Jameson builds a robot specifically to capture Spider-Man. Believing
    Spider-Man is a menace, he becomes obsessed with proving it. The
    robot malfunctions and becomes a danger to everyone. Spider-Man
    stops the machine and saves Jameson, despite his hostility.

8.  Never Step on a Scorpion 
    Villain: Scorpion 
    
    The Scorpion is created
    to defeat Spider-Man but becomes unstable and violent. His strength
    and agility rival Spider-Man’s, making him a major threat. He goes
    on a rampage through the city. Spider-Man eventually outmaneuvers
    and defeats him.

9.  Sands of Crime 
    Villain: Sandman 
    
    Sandman uses his shapeshifting
    abilities to commit crimes while impersonating Spider-Man. This
    turns the public against the hero. Spider-Man must both stop Sandman
    and prove his innocence. He exploits Sandman’s weakness to moisture
    to win.

10. Diet of Destruction 
    Villain: Green Goblin 

    The Green Goblin targets
    Spider-Man with psychological tricks and gadgets. He manipulates
    situations to weaken Spider-Man’s confidence. The battle becomes as
    much mental as physical. Spider-Man ultimately resists the mind
    games and defeats him.

11. The Witching Hour 
    Villain: Green Goblin 
    The Goblin returns with a
    more elaborate plan involving illusions and deception. He attempts
    to create chaos and confusion across the city. Spider-Man struggles
    to distinguish reality from trickery. He eventually sees through the
    illusions and stops the Goblin again.

12. The Kilowatt Kaper 
    Villain: Electro 

    Electro escapes prison and
    resumes his electrical crime spree. He seeks revenge on Spider-Man
    and escalates his attacks. The city’s infrastructure becomes a
    weapon in his hands. Spider-Man adapts and captures him once more.

13. The Peril of Parafino 
    Villain: Parafino 
    
    Parafino uses trickery and
    disguise to commit crimes. He manipulates others into doing his
    bidding. Spider-Man must untangle a web of deception to find the
    real culprit. He ultimately exposes Parafino’s schemes.

14. Horn of the Rhino 
    Villain: Rhino 
    
    The Rhino steals components for a
    powerful weapon. Spider-Man is weakened by illness but still pursues
    him. Despite his condition, he refuses to back down. He manages to
    stop Rhino through determination and strategy.

15. The One-Eyed Idol 
    Villain: Unknown mastermind 
    
    A mysterious idol
    hypnotizes Jameson into committing crimes. Spider-Man investigates
    the strange influence. He discovers the source of the mind control.
    The mastermind is stopped and Jameson is freed.

16. Fifth Avenue Phantom 
    Villain: Robotic criminals 
    
    Robots disguised as
    mannequins commit thefts across the city. Spider-Man tracks the
    unusual pattern. The mastermind uses technology to avoid detection.
    Spider-Man exposes and destroys the operation.

17. The Revenge of Dr. Magneto 
    Villain: Dr. Magneto 
    
    A magnetic villain
    causes chaos using powerful electromagnetic forces. Spider-Man
    struggles against attacks he can’t physically block. The city’s
    metal infrastructure becomes dangerous. He finds a way to neutralize
    Magneto’s powers.

18. The Sinister Prime Minister 
    Villain: Impostor 
    
    A political figure is
    impersonated as part of a larger plot. Spider-Man uncovers the
    deception. The plan threatens international stability. He exposes
    the impostor and saves the real leader.

19. The Night of the Villains 
    Villain: Organized crime group 
    
    A group of criminals uses theatrical disguises to commit crimes. 
    Spider-Man investigates their unusual methods. The illusions make them
    difficult to track. He uncovers their identities and stops them.

20. Here Comes Trubble 
    Villain: Trubble 
    
    A crime boss orchestrates a series of robberies. 
    Spider-Man follows the trail to the source. The
    villain relies on henchmen and strategy rather than power.
    Spider-Man dismantles the operation.
