Spider-Man (1967) – Season 3 Episode Summaries
40. The Dark Terrors

Villain: Fear-based criminals
A group uses fear-inducing methods to control and manipulate victims. Spider-Man investigates the psychological aspect of the crimes. The threat relies more on intimidation than strength. He exposes their tactics and stops them.

41. The Terrible Triumph of Dr. Octopus

Villain: Doctor Octopus
Doctor Octopus returns with a new plan and greater confidence. He believes he has finally surpassed Spider-Man. The confrontation pushes Spider-Man to his limits. He ultimately outsmarts and defeats Doc Ock once again.

42. Magic Malice

Villain: Illusionist criminal
A villain appears to use real magic to commit crimes. Spider-Man suspects there’s a scientific explanation behind it. The illusions create confusion and fear. He uncovers the trick and defeats the villain.

43. Fountain of Terror

Villain: Hidden mastermind
A mysterious location hides a dangerous secret tied to criminal activity. Spider-Man explores it and encounters traps and obstacles. The villain stays hidden while controlling events. Spider-Man uncovers and stops the plan.

44. Fiddler on the Loose

Villain: Musical criminal
A violinist uses sound-based techniques to disorient victims and commit crimes. The unusual weapon makes him hard to counter. Spider-Man must adapt to the sonic attacks. He captures the criminal.

45. To Catch a Spider

Villain: Multiple villains (team-up)
A group of criminals works together to capture Spider-Man. Their combined strategies nearly succeed. Spider-Man is pushed to think quickly under pressure. He escapes and defeats them individually.

46. Double Identity

Villain: Impostor
Another criminal impersonates Spider-Man, causing public distrust. Spider-Man investigates more carefully this time. The deception spreads confusion throughout the city. He quickly exposes and defeats the impostor.

47. Sting of the Scorpion

Villain: Scorpion
Scorpion returns stronger and more aggressive than before. He seeks revenge on Spider-Man for past defeats. The battle is intense and physical. Spider-Man manages to outlast and defeat him.

48. Trick or Treachery

Villain: Master of disguise
A criminal uses disguises to manipulate events and avoid capture. Spider-Man struggles to identify the real villain. The deception causes widespread confusion. He uncovers the truth and stops the criminal.

49. The Winged Thing

Villain: Flying creature
A mysterious flying creature terrorizes the city. Spider-Man investigates whether it’s natural or engineered. The attacks create panic among civilians. He subdues the creature and reveals the cause.

50. The Evil Sorcerer

Villain: Sorcerer (illusionist)
A villain uses theatrical “magic” to intimidate and commit crimes. Spider-Man faces bizarre and confusing situations. The sorcerer relies on fear and spectacle. Spider-Man exposes the illusion and stops him.

51. The Strange Case of the Cosmic Man

Villain: Cosmic entity (ambiguous)
A mysterious being with unusual powers appears in the city. Its intentions are unclear, creating uncertainty. Spider-Man investigates cautiously rather than attacking outright. The situation resolves as the entity departs or is neutralized.

52. Revolt in the Fifth Dimension

Villain: Extra-dimensional beings
Spider-Man encounters beings from another dimension. The conflict goes beyond ordinary crime and enters sci-fi territory. Reality itself begins to feel unstable. Spider-Man restores order and ends the threat.
