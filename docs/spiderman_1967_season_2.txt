Spider-Man (1967) – Season 2 Episode Summaries
21. The Origin of Spider-Man

Villain: Burglar
Peter Parker gains his powers after being bitten by a radioactive spider. At first, he uses them for fame and money rather than helping others. When he allows a burglar to escape, that same criminal later kills Uncle Ben. Peter tracks him down and learns the hard lesson that with great power comes responsibility.

22. King Pinned

Villain: Kingpin
The Kingpin secretly runs a large-scale counterfeit drug operation. Spider-Man investigates and uncovers how deeply embedded the crime network is. Kingpin relies on brute strength and calculated control rather than flashy gimmicks. Spider-Man disrupts the operation and forces Kingpin to retreat.

23. Swing City

Villain: Crime syndicate
A wave of coordinated robberies hits the city, all following a suspicious pattern. Spider-Man connects the crimes to an organized syndicate working behind the scenes. The criminals rely on timing and teamwork to stay ahead of the law. Spider-Man dismantles the operation by breaking their coordination.

24. Criminals in the Clouds

Villain: Airborne smugglers
A gang uses airplanes to smuggle goods, avoiding traditional law enforcement. Spider-Man is forced into an aerial confrontation, which limits his mobility. The criminals believe they’re untouchable at high altitude. Spider-Man adapts and brings them down mid-operation.

25. Diamond Dust

Villain: Jewel thieves
A crew of professional thieves targets high-value diamonds across the city. Spider-Man follows the trail of robberies to identify their methods. The criminals depend on precision and planning rather than force. Spider-Man anticipates their next move and stops them.

26. Spider-Man Meets Skyboy

Villain: Tech-based criminals
Spider-Man teams up with Skyboy, a young hero with advanced gadgets. They face criminals exploiting technology to commit high-tech thefts. Skyboy’s inexperience puts them both at risk. Spider-Man mentors him, and together they defeat the criminals.

27. Cold Storage

Villain: Freezing-tech scientist
Spider-Man is trapped in a freezing chamber by a scientist experimenting with cold technology. The extreme conditions disorient him and test his endurance. The villain plans to weaponize the freezing process. Spider-Man escapes and shuts down the operation.

28. To Cage a Spider

Villain: Hunter
A skilled hunter captures Spider-Man to prove he can defeat the ultimate prey. He uses traps and controlled environments to limit Spider-Man’s abilities. The situation forces Spider-Man to rely on intelligence over strength. He turns the hunter’s own traps against him.

29. Double Identity

Villain: Impostor
A criminal impersonates Spider-Man to commit crimes, damaging his reputation. The public begins to distrust Spider-Man. He investigates the impersonator’s methods carefully. Spider-Man exposes and defeats the impostor, restoring his name.

30. The Battle of the Humanoid

Villain: Giant humanoid robot
A scientist unleashes a massive robot capable of widespread destruction. Spider-Man struggles to fight something so powerful head-on. He searches for the robot’s control system instead. By disabling it, he neutralizes the threat.

31. Spider-Man Meets Dr. Noah Boddy

Villain: Dr. Noah Boddy
A mysterious scientist operates behind a hidden identity, conducting strange experiments. Spider-Man infiltrates his lab and encounters bizarre devices. The villain’s secrecy makes him difficult to track. Spider-Man uncovers his identity and stops his plans.

32. The Fantastic Fakir

Villain: Fakir
A magician uses illusions and trained animals to carry out crimes. Spider-Man must determine what is real and what is deception. The Fakir uses distraction to escape repeatedly. Spider-Man exposes the tricks and captures him.

33. Return of the Flying Dutchman

Villain: Pirate impostor
A ghostly ship appears, scaring people along the harbor. Spider-Man investigates and finds a human scheme behind the legend. The illusion is used to cover smuggling operations. He exposes the hoax and stops the criminals.

34. Farewell Performance

Villain: Stage magician criminal
A magician uses his performances as a cover for theft. Spider-Man goes undercover to uncover the truth. The villain uses misdirection and spectacle to avoid suspicion. Spider-Man reveals the trick and stops him.

35. The Golden Rhino

Villain: Rhino
Rhino returns, causing destruction while pursuing valuable materials. His brute strength makes direct combat risky. Spider-Man relies on strategy rather than force. He outmaneuvers Rhino and defeats him again.

36. Blueprint for Crime

Villain: Master planner
A criminal mastermind steals building blueprints to exploit structural weaknesses. Spider-Man follows a pattern of targeted locations. The villain avoids confrontation, relying on planning. Spider-Man predicts the next target and stops him.

37. The Spider and the Fly

Villain: Acrobatic thief
A nimble criminal matches Spider-Man’s agility and speed. Their encounters are fast-paced and unpredictable. The thief relies on precision and escape tactics. Spider-Man eventually outmaneuvers him.

38. The Slippery Dr. Von Schlick

Villain: Dr. Von Schlick
A scientist uses slippery substances to evade capture and commit crimes. Spider-Man struggles to maintain footing during fights. The villain’s gimmick creates chaos in every encounter. Spider-Man adapts and neutralizes the threat.

39. The Vulture’s Prey

Villain: Vulture
The Vulture targets important figures to lure Spider-Man into a trap. He uses aerial advantages to stay ahead. Spider-Man faces him in a dangerous sky battle. He defeats the Vulture and saves the victims.
